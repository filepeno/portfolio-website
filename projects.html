Mosquito Attack is a simple but addicting clicker game where the player must kill mosquitoes that are swarming around the character’s head while he is taking a pee. The idea is to kills as many mosquitoes as possible and not to get stung more than four times before the time runs out. The game was an individual project at KEA Copenhagen School of Design and Technology.

Code

JavaScript functions control the mechanics of the game, e.g. start and end of animations, keep track of points, lives, and time, and also play sounds. The game idea was first tested by a peer review of the paper prototype, and the logic of the game was then developed further with an UML Activity Diagram. Before coding, an UML State Machine Diagram was created to describe exactly what functions will do in detail. All code is written in Vanilla JavaScript.

Visual design and sound

The visuals of the game are all scalable vector graphics and designed in Adobe Photoshop and Illustrator. The characters are designed based on pointy and round shapes to express their personality. The style is inspired by flat design and Ryo Takemasa’s insect illustrations, among others. Sound also plays a major role, as it creates almost a horror-like atmosphere in the game. The soundtrack was built from a mix of sounds I recorded and sound libraries, and it can be turned on and off by the user.

Campaign video for a co-creation jewelry brand BYU Studio was a product of a group project which focused on creating a website and content for a start-up client at KEA Copenhagen School of Design and Technology. My main roles in the project were to research the brand and the market and be a part of developing a video both in ideation, filming and editing stages.

Video creation

The idea for the video was developed based on the research on the brand’s identity, values and target audience. It was important for us to capture the creative energy, warmth, the unique concept and values behind the brand. The video follows both how consumer can use the product and create their own designs and how the final piece of jewelry is created. The video also gives a personal look on the person behind the brand and portrays the target audience in sympathetic way.

Before filming, we created a storyboard that visualized the scenes in order to prepare for filming, and a video treatment that described the feeling and the idea of the video for the client. We filmed with two cameras to get varying angles and documentary-like, lively footage. Then we worked on editing and the soundtrack.

A group project where we re-designed a website solution for a Danish fashion and accessories brand SILFEN. My main roles in the project were to conduct research on users, product benchmark and design trends, and to code the product list page of the final web solution. Together with the group we worked on information architecture using sitemap and card sorting techniques, created personas and user stories based on user research, ideated on content and design, created a prototype in Figma, and tested the prototype with think-aloud tests.
